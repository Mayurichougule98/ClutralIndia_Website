Dance in Andhra Pradesh

Known for its rich culture, Andhra Pradesh is an exquisite state which has rich culture and this is prominent from an assortment of music, performing arts, drama, and dance which it presents before the entire world. Dance is considered to be an incredible form of expression which has been encouraged by many people in India since centuries. Kuchipudi, the world famous classical dance has evolved from Andhra Pradesh. Acknowledged as one of the best dances in whole world, Kuchipudi is considered to be replica of bharat natyam. Apart from Kuchipudi, there are few other dance forms and styles which took birth in this state. Perini, another impeccable dance form was also evolved and flourished here. This dance form represents warrior theme performed for Lord Shiva.

Kuchipudi - Folk Dance of Andhra Pradesh
kuchipudi is an excellent dance form which originated in Andhra Pradesh. Kuchipudi is not just a dance however it is a fine amalgamation of song, speech, gestures, and dance. A Kuchipudi dancer must have expertise in various languages, music, acting, dancing, and texts. Siddhendra Yogi in the 17th century started this dance form at the time of bhakti movement. Bhamakalpam Evolved from Kuchipudi, the dance form Bhamakalpam is a blend of drama and dance. Siddhendra Yogi found this devotional dance form which was originated in the 17th century. Bhamakalpam is an exquisite and fine example of feminine movements which are used in dance (lasya), opposite to the masculine movements which are presented in tandava of Yakshagana and Kathakali.

Burrakatha
Burrakatha is a unique folk dance of Andhra Pradesh. Also known as Jangam Katha, this dance form observes tales from our Indian mythology. In this dance performance, the main artist recites a tale, plays the music, and then dances on the tunes. There is a co-artist that beats the drums while speaks to him, thereby enriching many events from the story. Veeranatyam - The Dance of the Brave Veeranatyam means the dance of Lord Shiva which was done when Lord Shiva got infuriated with the humiliation and death of Sati. This dance form is also called the dance of destruction. This particular dance is even now practiced by the people who belong to Veeramusti community, that claim to be the offspring of Veerbhadra.

Butta Bommalu - The Dance of the Masks
A typical dance form, Butta Bommalu is very popular in West Godavari’s Tanuku region in Andhra Pradesh. In this typical dance form, the dancers wear a mask portraying different characters while entertain the people with their special delicate movements which are accompanied by non verbal or silent music.

Dappu - The Dance of Percussion
Dappu, a very lively dance form that evolved in Nizamabad District, is very exciting and pleasure to watch. Here the dancers wear colorful and attractive dresses while dance to the musical tones of harmonium, tabla and cymbals. In this dance form, the themes are generally based on different mythological stories. Tappeta Gullu - The Dance of the Rain God Considered as a devotional dance form, Tappeta Gullu is very popular in districts of Vizianagaram and Srikakulam. This dance form has tempo, rhythm, and vigor while is performed to please the rain God. In this particular dance form, the artists are found hanging drums around their necks and then produce beats with movements.

Lambadi - The Dance of Nomads
Linked with the Lambadi tribe, this dance form is performed to celebrate incredible sowing season or loaded harvest. The dancers in this dance form adorn themselves with costumes that have glass beads embroidery, ornate jewelry, brass anklets, and ivory bangles.

Bonalu - The Dance of the Balancing Pots Bonalu is a very special and unique dance form where the female dancers dance to match the rhythmic beats while balancing pots on their heads. Bonalu dance is performed to praise Mahankali, the Goddess of this village. Potharajus, a dance performed by males is done before the female dancers in the temple.

Dhimsa - The Dance of the Hills Tribes
Dhimsa is performed by individuals of different ages in the hilly region of Vishakapatam. This tribal dance is performed in the months of March and April i. e. Chaitra. The dancers dress in particular tribal dress and ornaments to perform this dance. Kolattam Kolattam is a dance which is performed with a stick and is replica to dance dandia of Gujarat. Also known as Kolkolannalu or Kolannalu, this dance form is generally performed during the village festivals. Kolattam observes a perfect blend of music, songs, and rhythmic movements.
